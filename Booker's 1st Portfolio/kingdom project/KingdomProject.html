Kingdom Project

Where can a man find peace in troubled time, without adding to the trouble through his misdeeds?

"Let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath/anger: For wrath/anger of man worketh not the righteousness of God"

These word were spoken in James, chapter 1:19-20; could it be that our quickness to become angry leads to the start of our troubles? How often do you become angry in a day? How many times does that anger lead you towards violence? What ways can you deal with your anger in a way that takes you out of the danger zone and allows you to concentrate on what the real issues are?

Take your time and meditate on this and consider what can be done to remove yourself from the reaction mode and enter in to the response mode. Plan how you will respond to negativity and conflict which will surely come in your life.

Reaction Mode

What is a reaction? For this lesson we will define it as a knee jerk. If someone hits your knee with a mallet your knee will automatically jerk. This is also true in our lives! If someone pushes the right buttons we normal react with a knee jerk. That is the angry and violent response that kicks in and leads us down dangerous paths. Over and over again no matter how much distruction this has already caused in our lives we tend to follow the same path.

How long will you follow the same path? Doing the same thing over and over again and expecting a different result is "insanity"! Are we truely insane? Are you still doing the same thing over and over and receiving the same results?

Listening and Contemplating

"Let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath/anger: For wrath/anger of man worketh not the righteousness of God"

Have you ever sat at the feet of an elder(mother, father, grandparent, etc...) and listen as they gave instructions or told a story? How much did you learn simply by listening and thinking on what they were saying? Did you contemplate on what they had told you or explained to you later in your time alone?

Taking the time to think on what we have heard and what we have learned can have an amazing effect on our understanding of the issue. We should always listen first, hold all questions and comments while we fully hear what is being said. Than we should take the time to think on what we have heard before giving a reply.

Taking action

After thinking on what we have heard we should follow the next few steps before replying. Ask yourself?

  1. What have I just heard?
  2. What does this mean to me?
  3. Am I angery about what has been said?
  4. What does it mean to the person I am conversing with?
  5. What is that persons,current status? Are they angery, hurt, confused, etc...
  6. When I answer, what will my words accomplish?
  7. Will my next action help or hurt the situation?

Growth

I can personally do whatever I wish but not all things are condusive to a peaceful life and I have the power to make this a fight or a moment of clarity. Not only for myself but also the other individual(s).

In the words of Smokey the Bear "only you can prevent forest fires". The words you speak can be the spark to life or death.

"If any man offend not in word the same is a perfect man, and able also to bridle the whole body" James 3:2
